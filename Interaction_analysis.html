Norman vs. GOMS Comparison Table

Comparison of Norman’s stages and GOMS concepts using the “Take a Photo” task on Xiaomi Redmi 14C
Norman’s Stage Real-World Example (Xiaomi Redmi 14C) GOMS Interpretation & Rationale
GOMS (1983) vs. Norman (1988)		 Alignment Analysis
Goal ↔ GOAL
(User intention) 		“I want to take a picture.” Goal: Capture-Photo.

This is the top-level objective in the GOMS hierarchy. 		Perfect Match
Both models begin with an explicit user intention.
Plan ↔ METHOD & Selection Rules

Cognitive decision before action 		Decision (Mental Planning):
“I’ll open the Camera app from the home screen.”

Alternative methods could include: • Lock-screen camera shortcut • Double-press power button (if enabled) 		Method:
Open Camera App → Frame subject → Tap Shutter.

Selection Rule (optional):
If phone is unlocked → use Home-Screen-Camera-Method. 		Conceptual Match

Norman’s “Plan” corresponds to GOMS “Method.”
Selection Rules are explicit in GOMS, implicit in Norman.
Specify Perceptual & cognitive work:

• User visually identifies the Camera icon.
• Inside the app, user frames the subject on the screen.

This involves perception, interpretation, and intention refinement. 		Not Explicitly Modeled.

GOMS assumes the user already knows what object to interact with.
KLM may include a mental operator [M], but it does not capture visual interpretation. 		Key Divergence

Norman explains perceptual understanding;
GOMS abstracts it away.
Execute ↔ OPERATOR Physical actions:

• Think about object to take picture
• Tap Camera icon
• Point camera at target
• Tap Screen for focused picture
• Tap Shutter button to take photo 		Operators:
• [M] Think about object to take picture -(~1.35s)
• [K] Tap Camera icon (~0.28s)
• [P] Point camera at target(~1.10s)
• [K] Tap Screen for focused picture(~0.2)
• [P] Point finger to shutter(~1.10s)
• [K] Tap shutter button(~0.2s)

KLM Sequence:
[M][K][P][K][P][K] 		Almost Match
Almost all motor actions are modeled in GOMS/KLM.
Perceive / Interpret / Evaluate Feedback loop:

1. Perceive: Screen flash + shutter sound
2. Interpret: “Photo has been captured.”
3. Evaluate: Thumbnail preview appears in gallery corner 		Not Modeled in GOMS.

GOMS assumes successful execution equals goal completion.
No evaluation or feedback stage exists. 		Fundamental Divergence

Norman emphasizes feedback and error detection;
GOMS ignores post-action evaluation.
